# Catalytic Pyrolysis of Lignin Using Mesoporous Zeolites

## Summary
Transforming industrial lignin into high-value aromatic chemical building blocks demands high catalyst selectivity. This paper explores modified ZSM-5 configurations optimized with hierarchical porous networks.

## 1. Introduction & Background
Lignocellulosic biomass stands as an abundant renewable carbon source [Martinez](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41560-025-0112). Traditional pyrolytic strategies yield highly oxygenated bio-oils requiring extensive hydrodeoxygenation. Recent efforts highlight zeolitic intervention [Wang & Zhao](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s10562-026-9).

## Methodology
Desilication of standard ZSM-5 was achieved via controlled sodium hydroxide alkaline treatment (0.2 M NaOH at 65°C). The resulting mesoporous framework was converted to its active H-form through ion exchange with ammonium nitrate, followed by calcination at 550°C.

## Discussion & Findings
The modified crystal architecture demonstrated a pronounced increase in accessible active pore sites, which dramatically shifted yield distributions toward target BTX fractions (benzene, toluene, xylene).

| Catalyst Base | Total Acidity (mmol/g) | Meso-pore Volume (cm³/g) | Liquid Fraction Yield (%) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Parent ZSM-5 | 0.82 | 0.05 | 32.1 |
| Meso-ZSM-5  | 0.61 | 0.24 | 48.7 |

This performance shift validates that micro-mesoporous hierarchies mitigate the rapid intra-crystalline diffusion limitations common to linear processing pathways.
