# High-Conductivity Solid-State Electrolytes via LLZO Doping

## Abstract
Solid-state sodium batteries require robust electrolyte interfaces to prevent dendrite growth. In this work, we present a novel aluminum-doped LLZO composition that optimizes ionic pathways. The synthesized material demonstrates an exceptional ionic conductivity threshold at room temperature.

## Introduction
The quest for safer energy storage has driven significant interest in solid-state configurations, as noted in previous industry reviews [Ahmad et al.](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.ensm.2025.10.042). However, structural degradation at the cathode interface remains a bottleneck.

![Figure 1: SEM Imaging of Interface Boundaries](https://storage.journal-assets.local/fig1.png)

By introducing subtle structural alterations, we can suppress secondary phase formations that traditionally limit performance [Lee & Kim](https://doi.org/10.1149/1.429482).

## Experimental Section
The precursor powders of Li7La3Zr2O12 were mixed via planetary ball milling at 400 rpm for 12 hours. Sintering was performed at 1100°C in an alumina crucible under an argon atmosphere. Inline titration techniques were leveraged to monitor active lithium loss during processing.

## Results and Discussion
Electrochemical impedance spectroscopy (EIS) confirmed a sharp drop in interfacial resistance. The full test metrics across variations are compiled below:

| Sample ID | Dopant Mol% | Interfacial Resistance (Ω·cm²) | Ionic Conductivity (S/cm) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| LLZO-Pure | 0.0 | 240 | 1.2e-4 |
| LLZO-Al05 | 0.5 | 85 | 4.8e-4 |
| LLZO-Al10 | 1.0 | 45 | 6.1e-4 |

The pristine configuration maintained stability over 300 continuous plating/stripping cycles without suffering a localized short circuit.
